By second grade<\/span>, Austrian students must learn cursive hand, based on the 1995 version of the <\/span>\u00d6sterreichische<\/span> Schulschrift<\/span><\/em>, or Austrian school script<\/span>. <\/span>The instruction of print style writing, however, is optional.<\/span><\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Bundesministerium f\u00fcr Bildung, Wissenschaft und Forschung<\/em> (BMBWF), or Federal Ministry of Education, Science and Research, is responsible for school as well as other levels of education. While most schools in Austria are public, private schools also exist. Schooling is divided into the following stages: Kindergarten<\/em> (under 6 years old), Volksschule<\/em> or primary school (1\u20134 grades), Allgemeinbildende H\u00f6here Schule<\/em> (AHS), or general secondary school, which has Unterstufe<\/em>, or lower, (5\u20138 grades) and Oberstufe<\/em>, or upper levels (9\u201314 grades). Education upto and including grade 8 is compulsory.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In 2003, the BMBWF published the last iteration of the Volksschul-Lehrplan<\/em>, or Elementary School Curriculum. The curriculum specifies that by the end of second grade, students should have legible and fluent handwriting based on the cursive \u00d6sterreichische<\/em> Schulschrift<\/em>, or Austrian school script, though they have leeway in how they adopt it. Schools may teach print style writing before cursive, but it is not mandatory.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
