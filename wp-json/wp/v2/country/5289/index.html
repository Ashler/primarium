The national curriculum in Slovakia provides a sample of fully-joined, cursive writing based on Zjednodu\u0161en\u00e1 psac\u00ed latinka <\/span>that must be used for handwriting instruction in schools, which usually begins in Grades 1\u20132.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

The Ministerstvo \u0161kolstva, vedy v\u00fdskumu a \u0161portu Slovenskej republiky<\/em>, or the Ministry of education, science and sports of the Slovak Republic, oversees education in Slovakia. The education system is divided into elementary school comprising Grade 1\u20134 (7\u201310 years old) and secondary school comprising Grades 5\u20139 (11\u201315 years old), and students may attend st\u0159edn\u00ed \u0161kola<\/em>, or middle school, for four years if they wish. Of these, elementary and secondary levels are compulsory. Education is fully public in Slovakia, but some private schools also operate in the country.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

In 2020, the latest iteration of the R\u00e1mcov\u00fd U\u010debn\u00fd Pl\u00e1n<\/em>, or Framework Curriculum, was published by the Ministerstvo<\/em>. It provides a sample of fully-joined, cursive writing based on Zjednodu\u0161en\u00e1 psac\u00ed latinka<\/em>, or Simplified Latin script, which was ratified by the government of erstwhile Czechoslovakia in 1932. According to the curriculum, students must learn to write based on this model, following its letter shapes as well as slant.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
