Cuba has a centralized, state-run and public education system, which is governed by the Ministerio de Educaci\u00f3n<\/em>, or Ministry of Education, also known as Minedu<\/em>. Education is divided into four stages: pre-school, primary (grades 1\u20136), secondary (grades 7\u20139) and pre-university (grades 10\u201312). Of these, primary and secondary stages are compulsory.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The Minedu<\/em> publishes a national curriculum, as well as textbooks and workbooks for all levels through the state-owned publishing house Editorial Pueblo y Educaci\u00f3n<\/em>. All schools must use these books. They are available in PDF format for each year on the ministry website, which is regularly maintained with its latest update in 2022. In addition to these, the Minedu also published Modelo de escuela Primaria Cubana, una propuesta desarrolladora de educaci\u00f3n, ense\u00f1anza y aprendizaje<\/em>, or the Cuban primary school model, a proposal to develop education, teaching and learning, in 2008.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
