The prevalent model for handwriting education in Poland is a loop-less, fully-joined vertical cursive, which can be traced back to primers by Marian Falski, the first of which was published in 1910.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

Education in Poland is governed by the Ministerstwo Edukacji Narodowej (MEN),<\/em> or Ministry of National Education, through its O\u015brodek Rozwoju Edukacji (ORE),<\/em> or Education Development Center. It is overwhelmingly publicly funded, and although private schools exist, they are a small minority. There are three levels in the Polish education system: przedszkole<\/em>, or pre-school education, for ages 3\u20136 years; szko\u0142a podstawowa,<\/em> or primary school education, for ages 7\u201315 years; and szko\u0142a \u015brednia<\/em>, or secondary education, for ages 15\u201320 years, with its first four years known as general secondary school. Education is compulsory starting from preschool and until graduation from general secondary school.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

As a result of the educational reforms of 2017, textbooks are now provided for free, including in e-book format, as part of two government programmes. They can be accessed through the educational platform of the Ministerstwa Edukacji i Nauki<\/em>, or Ministry of Education and Science, which also offers digital materials for primary and secondary school students. The textbooks published by the ORE include primary school primers, which were last published in 2020.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
