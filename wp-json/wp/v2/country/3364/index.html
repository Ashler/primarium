Students in Turkey learn handwriting beginning from Grade 1, using a model called Dik temel yaz\u0131<\/span><\/em>, which is upright, unjoined and geometric, based on the \u201cball and stick\u201d style. It was introduced in 2017, replacing a cursive first<\/span> approach.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n\n\n\n\n

In Turkey, the Milli E\u011fitim Bakanl\u0131\u011f\u0131,<\/em> or Ministry of National Education (MEB), is responsible for the administration of education, including the design of curricula.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

The education system is divided into the following levels: early childhood (0\u2013~5 years old); primary (6\u20139 years old), lower secondary (10\u201313 years old); upper secondary (14\u201317 years old), and higher education. Of these, primary and secondary levels have been compulsory since 2012. They fall under the purview of the General Directorate of Basic Education of the MEB, along with early childhood education. Primary and secondary education is state-funded, and offered free of charge. While fee-charging private schools also exist, they also fall under the charge of the MEB, and have no curricular freedom.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
