Handwriting instruction in South Australia (SA) is based on the latest edition of the state\u2019s Department of Education and Child Service\u2019s publication Handwriting in the South Australian Curriculum<\/em>. SA follows a progressive system that begins with simplified print style letters called \u201cbeginner\u2019s alphabet\u201d that are then joined to become the South Australian Modern Cursive<\/em> style. Even though the department made the typeface SA Modern Cursive<\/em> available in 2006, handwriting can be taught using any font that follows modern cursive conventions.<\/p>\n\n\n

After students learn to write simplified print letters in lowercase and uppercase, they are taught how to make exit strokes in letters in Year 2 (7\u20138 years old), and once they start showing signs of joining letters, proper linking techniques are taught from the beginning of Year 3 (8\u20139 years old). In middle school, students may be introduced to similar handwriting models and different writing tools for experimentation, and are encouraged to develop their personal handwriting style.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Lowercase letters are based on the foundation of a lightly sloped oval and the extenders are medium length. A distinguishing feature of the South Australian Modern Cursive<\/em> is its lack of loops. Uppercase letters follow print style conventions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This approach to handwriting is also used in some schools in Western Australia (WA).<\/p>\n\n\n\n
