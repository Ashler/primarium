Based on seven activity books, Nelson Handwriting is a handwriting education system for progressive modern cursive writing. It follows a three-step approach comprising basic, precursive, and cursive styles. Nelson Handwriting was developed by Alexander Inglis, Edward H. Gibson, and Douglas M. McIntosh in 1962, reformulated in 1984 for Thomas Nelson and Sons, and updated once again in 1997. Today it is published by the Oxford University Press.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
