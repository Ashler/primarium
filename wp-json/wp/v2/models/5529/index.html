Lateinische Ausgangsschrift<\/em> (LA), or Latin starting script, was introduced in Bundesrepublik Deutschland<\/em> (BRD), or West Germany, in 1953 by a decree of the Kultusministerkonferenz<\/em>, or the Conference of Ministers of Education. Bavaria adopted it in 1966. It was designed by Iserlohner Schreibkreis<\/em>, or the Iserlohn Writing Circle, a group of handwriting experts from across Germany.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
